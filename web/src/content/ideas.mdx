Stuck on what to build? Here's a menu — pick one and make it real.

## Starter ideas (a session)
- **Pod Doctor** — “Why is X failing?” → reads events + logs, names the root cause, suggests a fix.
- **Cluster Standup** — a one-paragraph health summary across namespaces, on demand.
- **Guided Remediation** — proposes the exact `oc` command to fix an issue; acts only after you approve.
- **Right-Sizer** — flags pods with no limits / over-asks and suggests requests & limits.

## Demo shapes
- **Live dashboard** — one URL showing cluster health, with a JSON endpoint behind it.
- **Chat plus evidence** — ask the copilot, then show the exact event/log/API evidence it used.
- **Approval flow** — the copilot proposes a patch, you approve, and the cluster changes visibly.
- **Before/after** — pre-break a workload, diagnose it, fix it, reload the dashboard.

## Stretch goals
- An MCP-powered copilot with typed cluster tools.
- A sub-agent “incident responder” that triages, then hands you a plan.
- A Slack/Telegram surface so you can ask Shifty from your phone.
- A custom OpenShell policy demonstrating least-privilege for the copilot.

## How submissions are judged
- **It works (40%)** — a live demo on your own cluster beats slides.
- **Useful (25%)** — solves a real operator pain.
- **Safe (20%)** — respects guardrails; writes are confirmed/scoped.
- **Polish & story (15%)** — clear demo, clear “why.”

<Callout type="tip" title="Demo tip">
Pre-break something small (set a bad image, remove a config, lower a memory limit), then let the copilot diagnose and propose the recovery live. A real before/after lands best.
</Callout>
