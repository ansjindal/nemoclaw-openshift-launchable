## The idea
You already have an agent running *inside* a real OpenShift cluster. Give it the ability to talk to that cluster and it becomes a copilot:

- **“Why is this pod crashing?”** — reads the pod, events, and logs; explains the root cause.
- **“What's unhealthy right now?”** — sweeps namespaces and summarizes what's not Running.
- **“Scale the frontend to 3 and roll it.”** — proposes the exact `oc` commands; you approve; it acts.

## The rules
- **Read freely, write carefully** — inspect anything; propose + confirm state-changing actions.
- **Guardrails are real** — bounded by OpenShift RBAC and OpenShell policy. Lean on them.
- **Demo-first** — a small copilot that does one thing well beats a grand design that doesn't run.

## The demo loop

A strong copilot demo has four beats:

1. **Show a real signal** — a failing pod, suspicious restart count, missing limit, or alert.
2. **Let the copilot inspect** — it reads cluster state with a scoped identity.
3. **Explain the diagnosis** — root cause, evidence, and confidence.
4. **Propose the fix** — exact command or patch, with human approval before any write.

That loop is what judges should see, whether your surface is chat, JSON, a dashboard, or a remediation form.
